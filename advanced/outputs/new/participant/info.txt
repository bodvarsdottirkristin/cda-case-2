Participant-level normalization removed most associations with Phase, Round, and Puzzler, but a weak-to-moderate cohort association remained (Cohort NMI ≈ 0.17). This suggests that even after removing individual physiological baselines, the latent space still contains session-level structure, possibly related to recording conditions, time of day, device effects, or cohort-specific signal characteristics. Importantly, participant normalization did not reveal phase-related emotional clustering, as Phase alignment remained close to zero.
